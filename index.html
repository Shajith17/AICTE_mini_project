  • Medication For Common Cold

    A common viral infection of the nose and throat. In contrast to the flu, a common cold can be caused by many different types of viruses. The condition is generally harmless and symptoms usually resolve within two weeks.

    Pain in the Muscles Cough Nasal Congestion Runny Nose Sneezing Loss of Smell

    The most commonly prescribed Medicine is Vicks 44 Cold and Cough

  • Conjunctivitis

    Inflammation or infection of the outer membrane of the eyeball and the inner eyelid. Conjunctivitis, or pink eye, is an irritation or inflammation of the conjunctiva, which covers the white part of the eyeball. It can be caused by allergies or a bacterial or viral infection.

    Pain in the eyes Irritation Redness of Eyelids Dryness Itchness Puffy Eyes

    Chloramphenicol. Chloramphenicol is usually the first choice of antibiotic and comes in the form of eye drops. It's available without a prescription from pharmacies to treat bacterial conjunctivitis.

  • Medication for Dysentery

    Inflammation of the intestines accompanied by bloody diarrhoea. Dysentery is most often caused by shigella bacteria (shigellosis) or an amoeba. Dysentery is often spread through contaminated food or water.

    Pain in the abdomen Diarrhoea Inability to empty bowels Indigestion Nausea vomiting or flatulence

    Antidiarrheal medicines include: Loperamide (1 brand name: Imodium). Bismuth subsalicylate (2 brand names: Kaopectate, Pepto-Bismol).

  • Medication for Stomach Flu

    An intestinal infection marked by diarrhoea, cramps, nausea, vomiting and fever. Stomach flu is typically spread by contact with an infected person or through contaminated food or water.

    Pain in the abdomen Diarrhoea Stomach cramps Belching Fatigue and Fever Dehydration

    Best course of medication is to provide Pepto-Bismol

  • Medication for Fever

    A fever is a temporary rise in body temperature. It's one part of an overall response from the body's immune system. A fever is usually caused by an infection. For most children and adults, a fever may be uncomfortable. A temporary increase in average body temperature of 98.6°F (37°C).

    Sweating Chills and shivering Headache Muscle aches Loss of appetite Irritability

    Medications such as paracetamol and ibuprofen may help to ease discomfort. Avoid giving children aspirin because this may cause a rare, serious condition.
